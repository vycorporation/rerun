# Selection is transient in the viewport and durable in the graph

Houdini Clone separates transient viewport and table selection from durable graph selection. Hover, click, brush, row selection, and immediate inspection targets are viewport or table state until the user explicitly commits them. Committing creates visible graph data such as selection, mask, or filter nodes by default; it edits an existing selection or mask node only when the target is unambiguous and the user chooses an update action. Committed selections must be based on record identity or derived record identity rather than row position, viewport pick id, or screen position. Downstream branches apply committed selections by identity intersection: records whose identities still exist remain selected, missing identities are reported in node info, and operators that destroy or replace identities must expose a record identity mapping or make downstream committed selections unavailable. Selection and mask operations are a first-class operator family for creating, combining, inverting, inspecting, and converting durable subsets, but selection and mask nodes do not filter records or carry appearance by themselves; filtering and styling require explicit consumer operators or actions. Selection-to-filter and mask-to-filter commands create visible filter operators wired to the subset and source branch; they do not mutate the subset node, hide records layer-locally, or materialize a copied dataset. Selection-to-style and mask-to-style commands create or edit visible style graph data or style bindings for highlighting, opacity, stroke, fill, labels, or other appearance. For routine selection, mask, filter, and style affordances, Houdini behavior is the default unless strong data kinds, record identity, graph visibility, viewer-agnostic output targets, or spatial/CV workflow constraints require translation. Houdini-style "group" remains a search and documentation alias for selection and mask concepts, not the canonical product term. Hidden layer-only selection state should not drive graph outputs.
